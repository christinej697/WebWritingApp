DLR 2: Dispelling the Ideal Independent Voltage Source Misconception

You will find at least one voltage source in almost every circuit you encounter in a basic circuit analysis course. Voltage sources are typically modeled as ideal independent voltage sources. To be sure, there are so-called dependent voltage sources, but such sources are depicted with a diamond instead of a circle. In the circuit we are considering, the voltage is an ideal independent source. Many errors in circuit analysis are made based on a misunderstanding of how to model an ideal independent voltage source. Let's make certain going forward that we understand the model.
